What are cookies?

A cookie is a small piece of data (text file) that a website saves on your device (computer, tablet, or smartphone) when you visit it. This allows the site to "remember" your actions and preferences (such as login, language, font size, and other settings) over a period of time, so you don't have to re-enter them each time you return to the site or browse from one page to another.

What types of cookies do we use?

We classify the cookies used on zelux-spark.blog into the following categories:

  • Strictly Necessary Cookies: These cookies are essential for the website to function. They allow you to navigate our blog and use its basic features. Without these cookies, providing certain services on the site may be technically impossible.
  • Analytical and Performance Cookies: These files collect anonymous information about how visitors use our resource. They help us understand which articles about AI and bots are the most popular and how users interact with the content. All data is collected in an anonymized form.
  • Functional Cookies: These cookies allow the site to remember the choices you make (such as your theme or region settings) to provide a more personalized learning experience.
  • Advertising (Marketing) Cookies: These files are used to deliver advertisements that are most relevant to your interests in high technology. They also help limit the number of times you see an ad and measure campaign effectiveness.
